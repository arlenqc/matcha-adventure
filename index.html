Care Guide

Preserve What You Love

Store matcha sealed in the refrigerator, away from light and moisture. Whisk your chasen in warm water before and after each use, and rest it on a kusenaoshi (whisk stand) to maintain its shape. Chawan should be rinsed with warm water only — never soap.

Our Story

Born Between the Rows

Origins

A Path Worn by Foot

Adventure Matcha began in 2018, not in a boardroom, but on a narrow footpath between tea rows in Uji, Japan. Our founder, Sora Hayashi, spent two years apprenticing on a small family farm — learning to identify the exact moment when shade-grown leaves reach their peak.

What started as a personal quest for authentic matcha evolved into relationships with five farms across Uji, Yame, and Nishio, and with artisans in Takayama and Karatsu whose families have practiced their craft for generations.

Every product we carry has a story, a maker, and a place. We never source from brokers or auctions. We know every hand that touches your matcha before it reaches you.

Patience Over Speed

Great matcha cannot be rushed. Shade-growing takes weeks. Stone-grinding takes hours per gram. We honor every step of the process and never cut corners for volume.

From Leaf to Cup

The Journey of Our Matcha

Shade Growing

For 20 to 30 days before harvest, the tea plants are covered with bamboo or synthetic screens. This boosts chlorophyll and L-theanine, creating the vivid color and smooth flavor.

Hand Picking

Only the youngest, tenderest leaves are selected by hand. Machine harvesting damages the leaf structure and introduces bitterness — we never allow it.

Steaming and Drying

Leaves are steamed immediately to halt oxidation, then air-dried and sorted. The result is tencha — the base material for matcha, flat and vibrant green.

Stone Grinding

Granite wheels turn at a slow, deliberate pace — no more than 40 grams per hour. Friction heat is carefully managed to preserve the delicate aroma compounds.

Ceremonial grade uses only the youngest leaves from the first harvest, stone-ground to an ultra-fine powder. It is meant to be whisked with water alone. Premium grade uses slightly more mature leaves and offers great flavor for both drinking and light lattes. Café grade is produced for blending with milk and other ingredients — it is robust and cost-effective for high-volume use.
Keep your matcha sealed in its original tin or an airtight container, stored in the refrigerator. Avoid repeated exposure to air, light, and humidity. Once opened, we recommend consuming within 4 to 6 weeks for peak freshness and flavor.
Always rinse your chasen with warm water before and after use — never use soap. After rinsing, shake off excess water and place it on a whisk stand (kusenaoshi) to air dry with the prongs facing down. This preserves the curved shape. Avoid leaving it standing in water, which will warp the prongs.
